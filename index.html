About.

I'm Shahinur Akther, a recent graduate in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology from the University of Chittagong. My academic work has centered on understanding gene regulation in disease — most recently through my undergraduate thesis on microRNA-21 and its role in breast cancer.

I'm drawn to research that connects molecular-level mechanisms to real clinical relevance, and I'm currently looking to build on this foundation — whether through graduate study, a research position, or opportunities in the biotech and pharmaceutical space.

When I'm not in the lab or reading papers, I'm usually exploring new ideas in genomics and looking for the next problem worth digging into. 💜
